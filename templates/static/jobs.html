UX/UI Designer

Sefaria is looking for our first full time designer to help create the best possible experience for textual engagement that the world has ever known.

About You

  • You're constantly struggling to make things simpler.
  • You can share with us a portfolio of products, experiences and graphics you've created and you can explain what motived the design of each.
  • You're familiar with the texts and technologies of Jewish textual learning.
  • Your best ideas stem from research; you know how to stage usability studies or conduct user interviews and observations to identify real needs ands pain points.
  • You think holistically about user needs and the full flow of user experience, but you're comfortable working with the fine details of pixels of palettes that make an application great.
  • You think text is beautiful. You could write a haiku about your favorite typeface.
  • You can read Hebrew and are comfortable designing layouts with Hebrew text.
  • You know how to validate your design decisions with data by crafting A/B tests and scrutinizing analytics.
  • You're an excellent communicator (in English) and value critical feedback and diverse opinions.

About the Role

  • You'll be the first person on our team working exclusively on design and you'll help set the priorities for time spent on new products and necessary redesigns.
  • You'll be working across a full spectrum of design levels (with a focus at the top):
    • High level product design for web and mobile.
    • Reusable interactions and widgets.
    • Visual components like graphics, icons, and palettes.
    • Informational layouts for communication.

Bonus Points

    • You've got a strong interest in and knowledge of Jewish textual learning.
    • You can talk for hours about the successes and failures of the Vilna Daf as a learning interface.
    • You're comfortable working in CSS and HTML when appropriate.
    • You like to hack out interactive designs yourself in Javascript.

Location

  • Remote OK, with a strong preference for San Francisco, New York or Jerusalem.

Compensation

  • Competitive Salary and Benefits.

Contact

  • brett@sefaria.org

Content Engineer

Sefaria is looking for a junior programmer to work on importing new texts into our database. The role involves writing scripts to parse text and data files and requires familiarity with traditional Jewish texts in Hebrew and Aramaic.

About the Role

  • Sefaria is building an online library of Jewish texts. We have people who OCR old books from which we need to get the digital text into our database. The process of parsing the unstructured digital text and posting them to Sefaria's database generally involves writing a small custom script which understands the structure to be extracted and translates it into JSON for posting.
  • One of the tricky pieces is that many commentaries need to be done in such a way that they connect properly to the books that they're commenting on. For example if there is a commentary on Torah, the book needs to be structured in such a way that each comment is linked to the underlying sentence in the Torah that is being commented on.
  • To facilitate this we have an API which can be used to upload books to the website. We also have lots of code from previous projects that has been used to upload various works. Most of our code is in Python, however you are not limited to Python if you prefer another language. Right now we have more projects than we can handle so we'd like to have someone we can turn to who will help us with the overload. Given that we want to increase our OCR speed in the coming months, we'll probably have a steady stream of projects going forward.
  • To do this job, you will need to understand the texts you're working with so you can make judgment calls on what should go where, or how different text sections should be broken up.

Sample Projects

  • Taking the OCRed text of a stand-alone book and having it automatically posted to the website.
  • Taking a commentary on a masechet of Talmud which has been 'tagged' (things like the dibur hamatchil and daf number have been marked), and uploading it to the website. You'll have to figure out where to make connections between the commentary and the underlying text.
  • Taking the XML output of another program, processing the XML, and uploading it as a JSON object to our website.

Terms

  • Contract based to start (hourly) with the opportunity to grow into a full time position.

Location

  • Remote OK, with a preference for Jerusalem/Israel.

Contact

  • ari@sefaria.org
