People

Brett Lockspeiser

- Co-Founder

Brett has been involved with designing and launching web applications since 2005, first as a product manager at Google then with a number of startups and not for profit organizations in San Francisco. At Google he led a team that collected hundreds of millions of historical articles to create the Google News Archives. Brett worked with the Kadist Art Foundation to create Kapsul, a tool for collaborative curating in contemporary art. Brett developed his appreciation and awe for the depth of Jewish texts while learning at Yeshivat Bat Ayin.

Rav Yehoshua Kahan

- Contributor & Advisor

Rav Yehoshua has been active in Jewish education in various settings for twenty-five years. Since making aliyah in 1990, Rav Yehoshua has served as Educational Director for the highly regarded Jewish identity program, Livnot U'Lehibanot and, subsequently, as Maggid Shiur (Lecturer) at Ohr Zarua, a post-high school program in Tzfat. He taught Gemara, Halachah, Chumash and Midrash at the Bat Ayin Yeshiva for ten years and at Sulam Yaakov Community Beit Midrash in Jerusalem, under whose auspices he served as director of Ashrei, a unique Torah beginner's program.

Rabbi Ellen Bernstein

- Organizational Development

Ellen is a rabbi, writer, organizational consultant and coach. Best known for founding Shomrei Adamah, Keepers of the Earth, the first national Jewish environmental organization, Ellen is particularly interested in how our relationship to place informs our lives and our language. She is author of several books on Judaism and ecology including The Splendor of Creation and Let the Earth Teach You Torah. Ellen believes that Judaism is a gift for all people and her deepest satisfaction is in helping others articulate their vision and manifest their work in the world.

Rabbi David Kasher

- Advisor

David is the Director of Education at Kevah, where he oversees Kevah's educational initiatives specificially focusing on the Kevah Teaching Fellowship. He attened rabbinical school at Yeshivat Chovevei Torah in New York, and was ordained there in 2007. Before joing Kevah, he worked as the Senior Jewish Educator at Berkeley Hillel, and as a lecturer at Berkeley Law.
