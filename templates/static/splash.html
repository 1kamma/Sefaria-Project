About Sefaria


The Sefaria Project is about building the future of Jewish learning in an open and participatory way.

We are building a free repository of Jewish Texts and their interconnections, in Hebrew and in translation. Our scope is Torah in the broadest sense, from Tanach to Talmud to Zohar to modern texts and all the volumes of commentary in between. Sefaria is created, edited, and annotated by an open community.

With these texts in hand, we are creating a novel, interactive interface for the iPad and Web which will allow students and scholars around the world to explore the interconnections in our tradition's core texts.

Judaism's core texts grew out of millennia-long conversations and arguments across generations. We are creating an open space for ancient conversations to continue in new ways, with new participants, new questions, and new layers of dialogue.

In the Public Domain

For the Jewish people, our texts are our collective treasure. They belong to everyone, in the public domain. It will take a huge amount of work to achieve, but we believe it's worth it.

Interconnected

Our tradition is inherently interconnceted. Dialogue, source citing, and reinterpretation have always been our way. We need to respond to the possibilities of new technology, just like the Vilna Gemara responded to the printing press.

Open to Reuse

We want to be resource without the red tape, whether it's copying a page of text for republication or downloaded all our data in public for research.

Community Driven

Our goal is to foster an open community of stewards who choose to take an active role in making our texts accessible and relevant to the future.

Infrastructure for Jewish Tech

All our code is released under an open source license, and we're offering services (like our Texts API) to any projects that can make use of them. No need to reinvent the wheel, we can share.
